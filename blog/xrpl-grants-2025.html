XRPL Foundation Announces $6M in Grants for 2025

The XRPL Foundation is funding projects focused on payments, tokenization, and developer tools. Canadian builders should apply.

The XRPL Foundation has announced $6 million in grant funding for 2025, targeting projects that advance real-world use cases on the XRP Ledger. This is one of the largest grant programs in the XRPL ecosystem—and Canadian developers, startups, and institutions should be paying attention.

Whether you're building payment infrastructure, tokenizing real-world assets, or creating developer tools—there's funding available. Here's what you need to know.

What the XRPL Foundation Is Looking For

The 2025 grant program prioritizes three core focus areas:

1. Payments & Cross-Border Settlement

Projects that leverage XRPL for real-world payment flows—remittances, merchant settlement, treasury operations, micropayments, or B2B payments. The Foundation wants to see projects that prove XRPL's speed and cost efficiency in production.

2. Tokenization & Real-World Assets (RWAs)

Projects tokenizing real-world assets on XRPL—real estate, commodities, carbon credits, invoices, or stablecoins. The Foundation is particularly interested in projects that bring institutional-grade compliance and custody to tokenized assets.

3. Developer Tools & Infrastructure

Projects that make it easier to build on XRPL—SDKs, indexers, explorers, wallets, analytics dashboards, testing frameworks, or no-code tools. The Foundation wants to lower the barrier to entry for new developers.

Grant Sizes & Structure

The XRPL Foundation offers grants in three tiers:

  • Small Grants ($5,000 - $25,000): For early-stage projects, MVPs, and research initiatives.
  • Medium Grants ($25,000 - $100,000): For projects with clear roadmaps, traction, and defined milestones.
  • Large Grants ($100,000+): For production-scale projects with significant ecosystem impact.

Grants are typically disbursed in milestones, with funding released as the project hits agreed-upon deliverables. The Foundation also provides technical support, ecosystem introductions, and marketing amplification for grantees.

Who Should Apply?

The XRPL Foundation funds a wide range of projects:

  • Developers: Building open-source tools, libraries, or applications on XRPL.
  • Startups: Launching fintech products, payment platforms, or tokenization infrastructure.
  • Researchers: Exploring novel use cases, security audits, or ecosystem analysis.
  • Institutions: Piloting XRPL for treasury operations, settlement, or asset tokenization.

Canadian builders have a unique advantage: Canada's progressive regulatory environment (first G7 nation to approve Bitcoin ETFs, clear crypto taxation) and strong fintech ecosystem make it an ideal testing ground for XRPL adoption.

How to Apply

Applications are submitted through the XRPL Grants Portal. The process typically includes:

  • Project Overview: What are you building? What problem does it solve?
  • Technical Plan: How will you use XRPL? What's your architecture?
  • Milestones & Timeline: What are your key deliverables? How long will it take?
  • Budget: How much funding do you need? How will you use it?
  • Team: Who's building this? What's your track record?

Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis. The Foundation typically responds within 4-6 weeks, with larger grants requiring additional due diligence.

Past Canadian Grantees (What's Possible)

While the XRPL ecosystem is still growing in Canada, there are precedents for Canadian projects receiving funding:

  • University research projects (York, UBC, Concordia) have received grants for blockchain security and consensus research.
  • Developer tools (e.g., XRPL explorers, wallet integrations) have been funded for Canadian teams.
  • Fintech pilots exploring XRPL for remittances or B2B payments have received early-stage support.

The opportunity is there—Canadian builders just need to apply.
