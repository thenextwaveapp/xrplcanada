How MoneyGram Moved $2.4B Using XRPL

Ripple's partnership with MoneyGram demonstrates real-world cross-border payment flows at scale—settlement in seconds, not days.

In 2019, Ripple and MoneyGram announced a partnership that would become one of the most significant real-world applications of blockchain technology for cross-border payments. Over the course of the partnership, MoneyGram moved $2.4 billion in transaction volume using XRPL's On-Demand Liquidity (ODL) solution.

This wasn't a pilot. It wasn't a proof-of-concept. It was production-grade infrastructure handling billions of dollars in real payments between real people—proving that XRPL could deliver on its promise of fast, low-cost cross-border settlement.

The Problem: Cross-Border Payment Friction

Traditional cross-border payments are slow, expensive, and opaque. MoneyGram, one of the world's largest money transfer companies, faced the same challenges as every remittance provider:

  • Pre-funded liquidity pools: Money had to be parked in destination countries before it could be sent—tying up capital and increasing operational costs.
  • Multi-day settlement: Traditional correspondent banking networks could take 3-5 days to settle cross-border transfers.
  • High fees: The cost of moving money across borders ate into margins and made small transactions uneconomical.

For remittance providers like MoneyGram, these inefficiencies translated directly into lower margins and less competitive pricing for customers sending money home to their families.

The Solution: On-Demand Liquidity (ODL) on XRPL

Ripple's On-Demand Liquidity (ODL) solution uses XRPL to eliminate the need for pre-funded accounts. Here's how it works:

How ODL Works

1. Source currency → XRP
MoneyGram converts the source currency (e.g., USD) to XRP on an exchange.

2. XRP transfer on XRPL
XRP is sent via the XRP Ledger in 3-5 seconds to the destination exchange.

3. XRP → Destination currency
The destination exchange converts XRP to local currency (e.g., PHP, MXN) for payout.

The entire process takes seconds instead of days, and requires zero pre-funded liquidity in destination countries. This unlocked significant capital efficiency for MoneyGram while reducing transaction costs.

The Results: $2.4B in Volume

Between 2019 and 2021, MoneyGram processed $2.4 billion in cross-border payments using XRPL. Key outcomes included:

  • Capital efficiency: No need to pre-fund accounts in every destination country—liquidity was available on-demand.
  • Faster settlement: Transactions settled in 3-5 seconds instead of 3-5 days.
  • Lower costs: Reduced fees for both MoneyGram and its customers.
  • Expanded corridors: ODL enabled MoneyGram to open new payment corridors (e.g., US-Philippines, US-Mexico) more efficiently.

MoneyGram CEO Alex Holmes noted at the time: "Ripple's blockchain-enabled payments have become one of our most efficient channels for converting currency."

Why This Matters for Canada

Canada is a remittance-sending country. According to the World Bank, Canadians sent over $30 billion in remittances in 2022, primarily to the Philippines, India, China, and other countries with large diaspora populations.

Canadian institutions—banks, credit unions, fintechs—could deploy similar infrastructure. XRPL's settlement speed, low cost, and capital efficiency make it a compelling alternative to SWIFT and correspondent banking for cross-border payments.

Canadian fintechs like Koho, Wealthsimple, and Neo are already innovating in digital banking. Imagine if they integrated XRPL for international transfers—instant settlement, transparent fees, and better FX rates for customers.

What's Next?

While the MoneyGram partnership ended in 2021 (due to regulatory headwinds from Ripple's SEC lawsuit), the infrastructure and learnings remain. Ripple's ODL solution continues to operate in multiple corridors, and XRPL remains one of the most proven blockchains for real-world payment flows.

For Canadian builders, this case study proves:

  • XRPL can handle production-scale payment volumes (billions of dollars)
  • Settlement speed matters—3-5 seconds unlocks capital efficiency
  • Blockchain-based payments are not just theory—they're operational today
